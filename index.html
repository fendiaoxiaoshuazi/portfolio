About Me


To me, Geographic Information Science (GIS) is the science that incorporate geographic and time factors into decision making. Furthermore, with the combination of GIS and Web 2.0, WebGIS can visualize the geographic information to public, and gather the volunteered geographic information from public.

I am a current graduate student pursuing a master degree within geographical information science at the University of Minnesota. I previously obtained my bachelor degrees in GIS and Geomatics with a minor in computer science. Over the past 4 years, my passion about GIS has continued growing, and I am especially interested in the field of spatial statistics, application development, and WebGIS. For my future career, I want to work for private agency and research organization as a GIS application developer, in the field of spatial statistics. Since GIS is an interdisciplinary science, the experience and knowledge accumulations are crucial for GIS professional. Owing to my background and experience, I am familiar with spatial analysis, spatial databases, remote sensing, web mapping, and the programming languages like C, Python, and JavaScript. I have hand-on experience with GI system, like ArcMap, ArcGIS pro, ArcGIS online, ENVI, ERDAS, and QGIS.

The Clifton StrengthesFinder identifies my top 5 themes as Input, Learner, Developer, Activator, and Communication, which I think well describe me. The themes Input, Learner and Activator work together makes me eager to learn more and turn thoughts into action. At my fourth year of undergraduate, I learned web design myself from zero, and I got a summer internship as mapping website designer. Now, I have created 3 mapping websites for carpool sharing, field photo exhibition, and analysis map exhibition. The themes Developer and Communication helps me work with group. I used to work for Dept. of Public Information of College Student Union at Wuhan University as the leader of technical group. I taught students in our faculty how to make high-quality slides, poster, and video; and I organized my group member to make the multi-media materials for student activities.

Thank you for your time! If you're interested in working together, contact me.
